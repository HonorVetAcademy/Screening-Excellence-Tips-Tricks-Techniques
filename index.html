Interactive Guide
Pre-Screening Form Walkthrough

Click each section to expand full guidance — purpose, required questions, recruiter tips, and the most common error for that section.

01
👋 Candidate Introduction & Contact Verification ▼

Establish rapport and lock in verified contact information for the candidate file.

  • Confirm full legal name and preferred name
  • Primary phone + backup phone + best callback time
  • Email address — confirm correct spelling
  • Current state of residence (critical for licensing)
  • Best timezone and availability window for follow-up
Recruiter TipAlways read back the contact info verbatim. One wrong digit in a phone number derails the whole pipeline.
Common ErrorAssuming the timezone from an area code — candidates may have relocated without changing numbers.
02
🪪 Employment Eligibility & Work Authorization ▼

Confirm the candidate is legally authorized to work in the US under their current status.

  • US Citizen, Green Card, H1B, OPT, or TN?
  • If visa: exact type, expiration date, any travel restrictions?
  • Documentation available for credentialing?
  • Any shift-type restrictions tied to work authorization?
Recruiter TipVisa holders need additional documentation upfront. Flag immediately to avoid delays at credentialing.
Common ErrorAssuming US citizenship without explicitly confirming — creates compliance exposure for the client contract.
03
📋 Nursing License Verification ▼

Confirm active, unrestricted RN licensure in the required state(s) before any submission.

  • Active RN license — which state(s)?
  • Compact license or individual state license?
  • License number and expiration date
  • Any license actions, restrictions, conditions, or encumbrances?
  • Has the license ever been surrendered, suspended, or revoked?
Recruiter TipAlways verify through the State Board of Nursing website before submitting. Verbal confirmation is not sufficient.
Common ErrorSubmitting a candidate to a client based on verbal 'yes, my license is active' without BON verification.
04
🏅 Certifications (BLS, ACLS, etc.) ▼

Confirm all required certifications are current, valid, and from accepted providers.

  • AHA BLS certification — exact expiration date (must be AHA, NOT Red Cross)
  • ACLS current? (Required: ICU, ER, PACU, CCU, CVICU)
  • PALS current? (Required: PICU, Pediatric ER, NICU adjacency)
  • CEN, CCRN, or other specialty certifications?
Recruiter TipBLS must be American Heart Association. Red Cross BLS is rejected by most travel contracts. Confirm provider explicitly.
Common ErrorMoving on after 'yes I have my BLS' without confirming the exact expiration date AND the AHA provider.
05
🩺 Clinical Experience & Specialization ▼

Assess clinical fit and verify the candidate meets minimum experience requirements.

  • Current specialty and total years of experience in that specialty
  • Secondary or cross-trained specialties (valuable for flexibility)
  • Level I Trauma experience or teaching hospital experience?
  • EHR systems: Epic, Cerner, Meditech, Allscripts — confirm proficiency level
Recruiter TipMost travel contracts require 1-2 years minimum specialty experience. Cross-specialty skill increases placement speed.
Common ErrorNot probing for EHR experience — facilities often reject candidates who have never used their specific system.
06
🔍 Criminal Background Check (BGC) ▼

Understand the candidate's criminal history within HonorVet's 10-year standard review window.

  • Any felony convictions in the last 10 years?
  • Any misdemeanor convictions? (DUI, theft, assault, fraud — explicitly named)
  • Pending charges or active investigations?
  • Any professional misconduct findings, board complaints, or terminations for cause?
Recruiter TipBe matter-of-fact and non-judgmental. A single misdemeanor from years ago is not always disqualifying. Always name misdemeanor types explicitly.
Common ErrorAsking only 'any felony convictions?' and stopping there. DUI and other misdemeanors surface in the full BGC and cause last-minute profile rejections.
07
⚖️ Malpractice History (Clinical Staff Only) ▼

Assess clinical liability exposure — asked only for clinical staff, not IT or non-clinical candidates.

  • Any malpractice claims filed against you?
  • Named as a defendant in any healthcare-related lawsuit, resolved or pending?
  • Any disciplinary actions initiated by a healthcare facility?
  • Any peer review findings or performance-related terminations?
Recruiter TipThis section is CLINICAL STAFF ONLY. Skip entirely for IT, admin, and non-clinical roles.
Common ErrorAsking malpractice questions to IT or non-clinical candidates — it creates confusion and damages rapport.
08
💰 Pay Rate & Compensation Structure ▼

Understand candidate pay expectations and explain HonorVet's compensation structure before any offer.

  • Current pay rate at present employer (W2 or hourly equivalent)
  • Target pay rate for travel assignment
  • Explain per diem structure: taxable base + non-taxable housing/M&IE stipends
  • HVT $300/month medical reimbursement benefit
  • Redacted W2 requirement for compliance
Recruiter TipAsk their target rate BEFORE stating HonorVet's range. Never disclose the client's bill rate to the candidate.
Common ErrorOpening with 'we offer up to $49/hr' — candidates anchor to the ceiling immediately and you lose all negotiating room.
09
📅 Work Availability & Shift Preferences ▼

Confirm start date flexibility, shift preference, and scheduling requirements before submission.

  • Available start date (2-4 weeks standard for travel)
  • Day, evening, or night shift preference?
  • 36 or 40 hours per week?
  • Weekend availability? Holiday availability?
  • Rotating shift flexibility? 8s, 10s, or 12s?
Recruiter TipNight shift and weekend flexibility dramatically increases placement speed. Ask specifically — do not accept vague 'flexible' answers.
Common ErrorMarking 'flexible' without confirming nights and weekends, then the candidate can't do nights after the client interview is scheduled.
10
🏥 Facility Requirements & Travel Compliance ▼

Confirm the candidate's readiness to meet facility-specific compliance and credentialing requirements.

  • Willing to relocate / travel? Geographic preferences or restrictions?
  • Housing preference: HVT stipend vs. company-arranged housing?
  • Drug screen consent (LabCorp Mon-Fri 11am-2pm only)
  • TB test, immunization records, and titers current?
  • NYCHH: fingerprinting required? NYP: Hep C 6-month titer required?
Recruiter TipNYCHH assignments require fingerprinting; NYP requires Hep C 6-month titer. Surface facility-specific requirements early or they delay start dates.
Common ErrorNot mentioning LabCorp's narrow window (11am-2pm Mon-Fri) — candidates miss it and the start date slips.
11
✅ RTR Authorization ▼

Obtain verbal Right to Represent before submitting the candidate's profile to any client ATS.

  • Explain RTR: exclusive authorization to represent this candidate for this specific position
  • Confirm no other agency has already submitted for this same opening
  • Get explicit verbal RTR — listen for a clear 'yes'
  • Confirm written RTR follow-up email will be sent same day
  • Log RTR timestamp in JobDiva
Recruiter TipRTR is the point of no return. Always get it verbally before ending the pre-screening call. Written confirmation goes same day.
Common ErrorHanging up intending to get RTR by email later, then submitting before it arrives — this creates dual-submission conflicts with other agencies.

10 Common Mistakes

Mistake 01
Revealing Client Name Before RTR
❌
Tap to reveal the fix →
CORRECTION
You mention the facility name during pre-screening to make the opportunity sound exciting.
✓ Never reveal the client or facility name until RTR is confirmed and documented. Lead with opportunity details, not the brand.
Mistake 02
Stating the Pay Ceiling First
❌
Tap to reveal the fix →
CORRECTION
You open with 'we offer up to $49/hr' before asking what the candidate expects.
✓ Always ask the candidate's target rate before stating HonorVet's range. Once you name the ceiling, all negotiation moves to that number.
Mistake 03
Skipping Malpractice for Clinical Staff
❌
Tap to reveal the fix →
CORRECTION
You complete pre-screening for an ICU RN without asking about malpractice history.
✓ Malpractice questions are mandatory for all clinical staff. Skipping this creates compliance exposure with the client contract.
Mistake 04
Asking Only About Felonies
❌
Tap to reveal the fix →
CORRECTION
You ask 'any felony convictions?' and stop — a DUI misdemeanor appears in the full BGC later.
✓ Always ask specifically about misdemeanors by name: 'Any DUI, theft, or assault misdemeanors in the last 10 years?' Both felonies and misdemeanors fall in HonorVet's 10-year window.
Mistake 05
Accepting 'Yes I Have BLS' Without Dates
❌
Tap to reveal the fix →
CORRECTION
Candidate says yes to BLS. You move on. BLS expired 5 weeks ago.
✓ Always confirm the exact expiration date AND that it is American Heart Association. 'Yes I have it' is never sufficient to proceed.
Mistake 06
Trusting Verbal License Confirmation
❌
Tap to reveal the fix →
CORRECTION
Candidate confirms their RN license is active. You submit without checking.
✓ Always verify through the State Board of Nursing website before any submission. Active verbal does not equal active license.
Mistake 07
Collecting Full SSN at Pre-Screen
❌
Tap to reveal the fix →
CORRECTION
You ask for the full SSN to 'start the file.'
✓ At pre-screening, collect only the last 4 SSN digits for identity confirmation. Full SSN is collected only after RTR, at formal application.
Mistake 08
Ending the Call Without RTR
❌
Tap to reveal the fix →
CORRECTION
The call goes well. You both seem aligned. You hang up planning to email for RTR later.
✓ RTR must be obtained verbally before ending the call. Say: 'Before I let you go, do I have your authorization to represent you exclusively for this position?'
Mistake 09
Not Explaining Per Diem Structure
❌
Tap to reveal the fix →
CORRECTION
Candidate accepts $47/hr assuming it is fully taxable. Their first pay stub creates confusion.
✓ Always explain the taxable/non-taxable split (base rate + housing stipend + M&IE) during pre-screening, before any verbal offer is made.
Mistake 10
Generic Availability Question
❌
Tap to reveal the fix →
CORRECTION
You ask 'when are you available?' and accept 'I am flexible' as an answer.
✓ Probe specifically: nights? weekends? holidays? 36 or 40 hours? Vague answers cause placement failure when facility schedule requirements do not match.

Quick Reference
Pre-Screening Cheat Sheet

Every card is a section of the HonorVet pre-screening form. Red border = Critical. Orange = Important. Review before every call.

📋 Candidate Intro CRITICAL
  • Full legal name + preferred name
  • Primary phone + backup + best callback time
  • Email address — spell it back
  • Timezone + state of residence
✅ Work Authorization CRITICAL
  • US Citizen / Green Card / H1B / OPT / TN
  • Visa holders: exact type + expiration date
  • Work authorization restrictions if any
📋 RN License CRITICAL
  • Which state(s) + compact vs. single-state
  • License number + exact expiration date
  • Any actions, restrictions, or encumbrances — verify via State BON
💉 BLS Certification CRITICAL
  • Must be American Heart Association — not Red Cross
  • Exact expiration date is mandatory
  • ACLS for ICU / ER / PACU / CVICU
  • PALS for Pediatric / PICU units
💪 Clinical Experience IMPORTANT
  • Current specialty + total years
  • Secondary / cross-trained specialties
  • Level I Trauma or teaching hospital?
  • EHR systems: Epic / Cerner / Meditech
🔎 BGC — Felonies CRITICAL
  • 10-year window — ask directly
  • Any felony convictions in the last 10 years?
  • Pending charges or active investigations?
🔎 BGC — Misdemeanors CRITICAL
  • 10-year window — name types explicitly
  • DUI / theft / assault / fraud misdemeanors?
  • Any terminations for cause?
⚖ Malpractice CRITICAL
  • Clinical staff ONLY — skip for IT / non-clinical
  • Any malpractice claims filed against you?
  • Named as defendant in any healthcare lawsuit?
💰 Pay Rate CRITICAL
  • Ask candidate target FIRST — never state ceiling first
  • Explain per diem split: taxable base + housing + M&IE
  • $300 HVT monthly medical reimbursement benefit
  • Never disclose the client bill rate
📅 Availability IMPORTANT
  • Target start date (2-4 weeks standard)
  • Shift preference: Days / Evenings / Nights
  • Night, weekend, holiday flexibility?
  • 36 or 40 hours per week?
💊 Drug Screen IMPORTANT
  • LabCorp consent required
  • Monday-Friday 11:00 AM to 2:00 PM ONLY
  • Valid government-issued photo ID required
🏠 Housing IMPORTANT
  • HVT housing stipend OR company housing
  • Geographic preferences or hard restrictions
  • Solo travel or travel with a companion?
🏢 Facility-Specific CLIENT
  • NYCHH: fingerprinting required before start
  • NYP: Hepatitis C 6-month titer required
  • Confirm all facility requirements early in process
📝 RTR Authorization CRITICAL
  • Verbal RTR must be obtained before ending the call
  • Written RTR follow-up email same business day
  • Log in JobDiva immediately — timestamp required
  • No dual submissions — confirm exclusivity
👤 References BEST PRACTICE
  • 2-3 professional references required
  • Direct supervisors or charge nurses only
  • Personal references are not accepted
🔁 Follow-Up BEST PRACTICE
  • 48-72 hour follow-up if no response
  • Every follow-up must carry new information
  • Never follow up with just checking in
